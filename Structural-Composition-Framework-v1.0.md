# Structural Composition Framework

Version 1.0  
Visual Engine Optimization (VEO)  
Specification Baseline — Version 1.0  

---

## 1. Definition of Structural Composition  

Structural Composition is defined as the degree to which visual elements are organized in a manner that enables consistent interpretation of relationships, hierarchy, and grouping by an AI-driven system.  

Structural Composition operates on detected and recognized elements.  

---

## 2. Composition Factors  

### 2.1 Spatial Hierarchy  

The arrangement of elements in a manner that reflects importance or priority.  

Includes:  
- Size differentiation  
- Positional prominence  
- Visual weighting  

Failure Condition:  
- No distinguishable hierarchy between elements  

---

### 2.2 Element Grouping  

The organization of related elements into identifiable clusters.  

Includes:  
- Logical grouping  
- Proximity-based association  
- Consistent alignment  

Failure Condition:  
- Related elements cannot be grouped or are ambiguously associated  

---

### 2.3 Segmentation Clarity  

The separation of distinct visual regions within the content.  

Includes:  
- Section boundaries  
- Content partitioning  
- Visual separation  

Failure Condition:  
- Boundaries between regions are unclear or overlapping  

---

### 2.4 Positional Consistency  

The predictable placement of elements within the layout.  

Includes:  
- Alignment consistency  
- Repeated structural patterns  
- Stable positioning  

Failure Condition:  
- Element placement is inconsistent or unpredictable  

---

## 3. Composition Conditions  

Visual content is considered structurally coherent when:  

- Hierarchy is identifiable  
- Related elements are grouped logically  
- Segmentation is clear  
- Layout structure is consistent  

---

## 4. Structural Failure Conditions  

Visual content is considered structurally degraded when:  

- Hierarchy cannot be determined  
- Grouping is ambiguous  
- Segmentation is unclear  
- Layout is inconsistent or disordered  

---

## 5. System Role  

Structural Composition supports:  

- Structural Coherence  
- Contextual Alignment  

It operates after:  

- Detectability  
- Recognizability  

Structural failure reduces the reliability of contextual interpretation.  

---

## 6. Boundary  

This specification:  

- Does not define design aesthetics  
- Does not define user experience preferences  
- Does not define platform-specific layout rules  

It defines structural organization for machine interpretation only.
